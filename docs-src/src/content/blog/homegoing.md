I just finished it today and honestly I can say that it's a rare book that touched me.
I have read other books that tell the story of people of the same family over multiple generations,
namely _Pachinko_ by Min Jin Lee.

Here we are following the parallel stories of the descendants of two half-sisters from Ghana, one
married to a slaver, one sold as a slave herself.

The book is relatively short, the time spent on a specific character as well, yet I felt drawn to
each one of them and impatient to discover what happened to them when we follow their children which
shows a real talent in the writing.
Overall it's a really good read that I highly recommend.

Now, I'm not a literary critic or anything and would be hard pressed to give you a quality analysis
of the writing, but there are a few things I thought about and that I wanted to share.

Reading this I realized how little I know about History. I think of myself as a person who likes to
explore the past and yet I struggled through this book. References were being made to events,
tribes, locations, most of which rang no bell and it made me realize how much of a blindspot I have.

Another thing I noticed is how much I felt close to the feelings of some of the characters despite
being from a whole other ethnicity.
I might not relate to most of the systemic racism Black people were victims of but some things I can
understand.

The description of the split identities you grow up with when your parents are not from the place
you lived most of your life, how you feel like you belong nowhere, not in your homeland, not in your
current country, always being conscious of your difference, it all felt so right.

It's a masterpiece and one of my literary _coup de coeur_ of the year.
